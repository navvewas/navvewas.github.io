Navve Wasserman

I'm a PhD student in computer science and machine learning at the Weizmann Institute of Science. I work at the intersection of computer vision, large language models, and computational neuroscience, with a focus on: brain-to-model alignment, fMRI interpretability, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG).

Recently, I've been developing large-scale fMRI interpretability frameworks (e.g., BrainExplore / Brain-IT) and robust multimodal RAG rerankers (e.g., DocReRank and Real-MM-RAG).

Research

I am interested in computer vision, deep learning, and computational neuroscience. My work spans: (i) large-scale, brain-aligned representations for images and language, (ii) retrieval-augmented generation and reranking for real-world documents, and (iii) interpretable models and evaluation for perceptual tasks (image quality, emotion, memorability, etc.). Some projects are highlighted.

DocReRank: Single-Page Hard Negative Query Generation for RAG Rerankers
Navve Wasserman,
EMNLP, 2025
project page / arXiv

We introduce a framework for generating hard negative queries directly from single documents, enabling robust training of document-level rerankers for retrieval-augmented generation.

Real-MM-RAG: Robust Multimodal Retrieval-Augmented Generation in the Wild
Navve Wasserman,
ACL, 2025
project page / arXiv

A benchmark and pipeline for evaluating RAG systems with real-world multimodal documents: messy PDFs, images, and long-context reasoning.

BrainExplore: Large-Scale fMRI Concept Discovery for Vision Models
Navve Wasserman,
In progress
project page / arXiv

A unified pipeline that decomposes fMRI activity, retrieves visual concepts, and scores brain-model alignment across ROIs, patterns, and large concept dictionaries.
